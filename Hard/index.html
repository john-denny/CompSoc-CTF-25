The Ballad of Cathal Lawlor

Oh, gather 'round and hear the tale,
Of Cathal, stout and true,
The auditor of CompSoc's halls,
A name both old and new.

With ledger firm and keen of eye,
He kept the records clear,
No errant coin, no misplaced dime,
Escaped his watchful ear.

In meetings lit by flickering screens,
He sat, both calm and wise,
Deciphering the spreadsheets' truths,
While others theorized.

"Secure the budget!" he would call,
"Ensure the funds align!
For every pizza, LAN, or prize,
The numbers must combine."

But Cathal was no mere clerk, you see,
He joined the games and fun,
A master of the CTFs,
A hacker's chosen one.

He cracked the code, he solved the clue,
He led the daring raids,
While balancing the books with care,
In spreadsheets he had made.

The lore of Cathal soon did spread,
Through every campus hall,
A hero with a keyboard's might,
And balance sheets for all.

So raise a glass to Cathal's name,
The king of budget lore,
The auditor of CompSoc's reign,
A legend evermore.
